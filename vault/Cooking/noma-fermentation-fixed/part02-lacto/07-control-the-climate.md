# Control the Climate

Most lacto-fermentations will function just fine at an ambient room temperature of about 21°C/70°F, but at Noma, we keep most of our lacto-fermentations in a room set to 28°C/82°F. We find this to be the ideal temperature for speedy fermentation, while avoidingtoomuch bacterial activity, which can generate off-flavors. Lacto-fermentation will also take place in the refrigerator, albeit at a far slower pace.

One thing to note: If you want to prevent pickles from getting mushy, ferment them away from heat. There are natural enzymes in vegetables that will break them down more rapidly at higher temperatures. If you’re especially concerned with maintaining pickle crunchiness, adding tannic plant leaves—like grape or horseradish leaves— to your brine or using mineral-rich unrefined sea salts or alum can reinforce the pectin in the plant walls and keep them snappy.
